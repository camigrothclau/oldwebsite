What do you find as being important when practicing UX Research?

First of all, know your methods and research techniques, choose your research method based on the project goal and be careful that you keep that goal in mind throughout the whole process. If you loose track of the project goal and purpose you end of with (probably interesting) results, that don’t answer the questions you had to begin with. And of course, be aware of bias! Did you affect the respondent’s answers by asking the interview question in a tricky or leading way? Second, how and where do you retain your research insights within the company and how can different stakeholders access specific insights when they need to? It shouldn’t be time consuming for stakeholders to find the insights they are looking for. Forget about long reports but keep it as short and clear as possible - and think about stakeholder roles; a marketing colleague is probably interested in other insights than developers and c-level something third etc.
